The Ability to Access

Accessibility is a broad descriptor generally misapplied to a narrow set of users. Every person has accessibility needs which evolve. It's not just a matter of empathy for those unfortunate enough to have a visible disability. Accessible technologies are good technologies which benefit the able-bodied as much or more as the people they were designed to assist. Here is a selection of links which support that assertion.

Accessibility | MDN

Accessibility (often abbreviated to A11y — as in, "a", then 11 characters, and then "y") in web development means enabling as many people as possible to use websites, even when those people's abilities are limited in some way.

Not fan of describing accessibility solely in terms of peoples limitations but this resource is fantastic and vital to understanding how to apply assistive technologies in many different ways.
